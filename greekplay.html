Sowmya Medabalimi presents a Greek play by Sophocles.

Philoctetes by Sophocles

Neoptolemus

I will speak, and thou shalt mark me. All this time I have been tortured with remorse, because, Beguiled by those I should not have obeyed, I have deceived thee and brought all this wrong On thee, thou injured one! Forgive me, son Of Poeas!—let me hear thee say, “I forgive thee.”

Philoctetes

_Philoctetes frowns, confused._
Say what? Thou talk'st, methinks, beyond my ken.

Neoptolemus

I will repeat it. All my evil act Was wrought at the behest of these, not mine.

Philoctetes

_Philoctetes clenches his fists._
How? By whose urging couldst thou do such things?

Neoptolemus

The Atreidae and Odysseus pressed it on me.

Philoctetes

_Philoctetes raises his voice._

Neoptolemus

It is their cause that now compels my going.
